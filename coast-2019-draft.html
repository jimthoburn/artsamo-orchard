COAST (DRAFT)

On Sunday, September 15, sections of Ocean Avenue, Colorado Esplanade, and Main Street (that’s two miles!) will be closed to cars and open to people of all ages and abilities to bike, skate, scoot, run, walk, and roll. All are invited to participate in this free city celebration of a more people-powered and sustainable Santa Monica. 

Leading up to the big day, COAST invites everyone to create and decorate your favorite transportation mode of choice, including yourself! Then participate in this carnival of wheels, rigs, wings, and feet and win prizes ranging from $100 to $500 for creativity and innovation!

From August 28 to September 14, free artist-led workshops provide inspiration and materials for you and your friends, family and neighbors to create and decorate costumes, conveyances, walking pieces, and devices.

COAST invites everyone to be part of a carnival of wheels, rigs, wings, and feet! Ride your bike, skate around, take a stroll, roll through, and check things out - COAST will have live music, performances, lots of food to eat, and artsy activities.

    10:00am-4:00pm on Ocean Avenue between Arizona and Wilshire

Come put on your roller skates and go rolling around the rink or just sit and reminisce about the solid sounds of yesteryear. Don’t have roller skates? No worries, we have pairs you can borrow at no cost.

    10:00am-4:00pm on Main Street between Bay Street and Pico

    10:00am-4:00pm on the lawn in front of the Santa Monica Civic Auditorium

    Discover how much more you can do for the environment, help artist Peter Tigler complete a giant fingerprint mural and groove to the music of local DJ's, Venice Beats.

    10:00am-4:00pm on Ocean Avenue between Colorado and Broadway

    Bike training course to improve your skills and learn safety precautions with Santa Monica Spoke. Decorate your own helmet, or create a sash to wear in honor of the 100th anniversary of the League of Women Voters.

  • Demonstration Cycle Track

    10:00am-4:00pm on Ocean Avenue between California Incline and Wilshire Boulevard

    Temporary protected bike lane with banners by world-renowned artist and award-winning kitemaker, Jose Sainz.

Artist-led Workshops

Between August 28 and September 14, join free artist-led workshops where inspiration and materials will enable you to create your showpiece for COAST, on September 15. Collaborate with friends, family, and neighbors to decorate whatever your way of getting around is (whether it’s with wheels or not!) and create a larger than life moving installation. Bring, wear and ride your creations to the streets on event day, where there will be opportunities to win prizes for creativity and innovation. Attend a workshop and make your masterpiece!

All workshops are free will take place in the Santa Monica Civic Auditorium unless otherwise stated.

  • Decorate your helmet, hat or head with Artist Marianne Sadowski

    Create head-ware that reflects you and your favorite way to get around. All kinds of fabulous and fun materials will allow you to create whatever you imagine.

    Check out the workshop schedule below for details!

  • Sea to Sky: Giant Puppet Making with Beth Peterson

    This workshop is inspired by Tongva and Chumash canoes, the whales of the Pacific, and other animals and birds of our coast. You can join with others to create a giant, communal puppet or large group-powered creation, or create a solo piece. The larger than life people-powered post-fossil fuel creations will soar and sail through the streets at COAST powered by groups and individuals on foot, bicycle, roller skates, strollers, scooters and skateboards.

    Check out the workshop schedule below for details!

  • People-Powered Transportation Revolution with Artist Kiel Johnson

    Use cardboard, paint, and other fun materials to build a sailboat, chariot, train, bus, gondola, raft, jet pack or anything you can imagine to transport you through the streets of COAST.

    Check out the workshop schedule below for details!

  • What’s your sign? Sign Making with Artist Marianne Sadowski

    Say it with a sign! Express yourself, your family, organization or community with a handmade sign created from stencils, images and cardboard and then carry it or affix it to your conveyance along the COAST route.

    Check out the workshop schedule below for details!

  • Mask Making and Collaborative Dance with Mimi Haddon

    Combine cardboard, paint, yarn, fabric and other objects to create a mask that uniquely expresses you, then join in inventing a collaborative dance that will respond to the building and site. Keep your mask and wear it to COAST on September 15. (Wear clothing to move in for workshop.)

    Check out the workshop schedule below for details!

COAST Contest

This year, COAST will be a pageant of creativity by participants who can win cash prizes ranging from $100 to $500 in the COAST Contest, open accessible and free to all. The contest judges will be looking for creativity and innovation, as well as entrants who bring the COAST ethos of sustainability, people-powered mobility and the spirit of Santa Monica to their submission. 

Categories:

  • Most COAST - the single best representation of the COAST ethos
  • Boldest Mobility Solution
  • Most Creative or Innovative
  • Best Use of Recycle Materials
  • Best Youth Entry
  • Best Group Effort

How to Enter

Create or make your own float, costume, headpiece, people-powered conveyance, or large-scale human-powered surprise to bring to COAST. 

Drop-in to any of the free workshops to hone your work, get access to free materials, work with our in-house artists, or find inspiration from watching your neighbors and friends create. 

Finish your creation and bring it along with your friends and family to COAST on September 15

Register online or sign-up to enter the contest when you get to COAST on September 15. Once registered, you'll get assigned a number/bib to wear for judging. 

Go to the review area between 10AM - 3PM so that you can be seen by our esteemed panel of judges. 

Local Business

Businesses along the route are invited to participate!

Follow the do's and don'ts outlined below for ideas on how you can activate the street in front of your establishment during COAST. Please submit your ideas to the event production team, Community Arts Resources (CARS) by August 23. Street activities not pre-approved may receive a citiation from the City of Santa Monica.

For more details on how you can participate, please contact CARS at kai@carsla.net or (213) 355-8506.

DO

  • Open your doors to the public during COAST!
  • Host a sidewalk sale in front of your store.
  • Offer a COAST special. Sign up an publicize your special here. 
  • Develop an interactive activity that engagess attendees. Contact us for ideas!

DON’T

  • Block the entire sidewalk; a clear walking path must be maintained.
  • Post stationary signs in the public right-of-way. 
  • Have sales beyond the sidewalk. Sales are not permitted in the street. 

Event Mission

Mobility:

COAST celebrates all the ways people get around without cars. 

The goal is to lessen our dependence on cars in order to advance the health of our environment, our bodies, and our bank accounts. 

People-powered = power to the people!

Community:

COAST creates a carfree public space where the community can engage with each other and the city around them in new and creative ways.

The goal is to inspire people to imagine and experience new ways of being and moving in the streets and to connect and build, together, lasting change within the community.

Creativity:

COAST participants make the event magic through their self-expression and collaboration. 

The goal is to support and facilitate the community’s collaborative imagination. We want you (yes, you!) to make something awesome!

Getting to COAST on 9/15

Hop on the Bus

Attending COAST just got easier this year with the exciting offer of free rides on all Big Blue Buses across all routes the entire day of the event. Take routes 1, 2, 3, Rapid 3, 5, 7, Rapid 7 8, 9, an 18 all serve Downtown Santa Monica. With so many options, one probably goes by you! Find your route here. 

Metro Buses 4/704, 534, 720, and 733 all drop you off within walking distance of the route.

Ride the Expo Line

Metro Rail drops you off directly on the route at Colorado/4th Street. Use the Metro Trip Planner to find the best route.

Check out Santa Monica’s Bike Map to chart your best path to the event. Or use Google Maps if coming from outside the city.

Accessibility

COAST is a wheelchair accessible and ADA compliant event. Approximately 2 miles of streets will be closed to vehicles so that all sorts of wheeled devices can enjoy the space together. The streets, sidewalks, Colorado Esplanade, Third Street Promenade, Santa Monica Place, Palisades Park, and other programmed areas are accessible. For some programming, travel over grass will be required. 

All parking lots have ADA accessible spots. See above for the list of parking lot closures. 

Public transportation including Big Blue Bus, Metro buses, and Metro Expo Line are accessible. Please note some buses will be rerouted due to street closures from the event. 

All public and portable restrooms at the event include accessible options. 

Safety

Rules

Share the Road – While cars are not allowed, most other forms of non-motorized transportation are. Be mindful of others enjoying the route with you!

Car Crossings – At a few locations along the route we’ll have designated intersections for cars to cross. Rides and pedestrians must stop at these crossings when told to do so. 

Helmets for Children – All children (18 and under) are required by law to wear a helmet if they are on a bike, scooter, skateboard, roller blades, or any other similar type of transportation. 

Electric Scooters/Bikes – Any electric assisted bicycles or scooters are limited to 5 miles per hour within the event area. All shared devices will have their throttle automatically adjusted. 

Safety

The Santa Monica Police Department provides security to the COAST event. Additionally, we’ll have emergency services stationed throughout the event. 

In the case of an accident, report the incident immediately to the nearest event staff member, emergency personnel, or uniformed officer. 

Report any suspicious activity or packages to the nearest staff member or uniformed officer. 

Staff can be identified by the t-shirt that says STAFF on the back. Volunteers can be identified by the t-shirt that says VOLUNTEER on the back.

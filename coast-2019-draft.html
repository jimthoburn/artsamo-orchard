COAST invites everyone to create and decorate your favorite people-powered transportation mode of choice, including yourself! Then participate in this carnival of wheels, rigs, wings, and feet and win prizes ranging from $100 to $500 for creativity and innovation! Free artist-led workshops provide inspiration and materials for you and your friends, family and neighbors to create and decorate costumes, conveyances, walking pieces, and devices. 

Sections of Ocean Avenue, Colorado Esplanade, and Main Street (that’s two miles!) will be closed to cars and open to people of all ages and abilities to bike, skate, scoot, run, walk, and roll. All are invited to participate in this free city celebration of a more people-powered and sustainable Santa Monica. 

Artist-led Workshops

Leading up to the event, join free artist-led workshops where inspiration and materials will enable you to create and decorate whatever your way of getting around is – whether it’s with wheels or not! And collaborate with friends, family and neighbors to create a larger than life moving installation. Bring, wear and ride your creations to the streets on event day, where there will be opportunities to win prizes for creativity and innovation. Attend a workshop and make your masterpiece!

All workshops will take place in the Santa Monica Civic Auditorium unless otherwise stated.

Contest

This year, COAST will be a pageant of creativity by participants who can win cash prizes ranging from $100 to $500 in the COAST Contest, open accessible and free to all. The contest judgest will be looking for creativity and innovation, as well as entrants who bring the COAST ethos of sustainability, people-powered mobility and the spirit of Santa Monica to their submission. 

Categories:

  • Most COAST - the single best representation of the COAST ethos
  • Boldest Mobility Solution
  • Most Creative or Innovative
  • Best Use of Recycle Materials
  • Best Youth Entry
  • Best Group Effort
  • People's Choice

How to Get Involved

Create or make your own float, costume, headpiece, people-powered conveyance, or large-scale human-powered surprise to bring to COAST. 

Come to any of the free drop=in workshops to hone your work, get access to free materials, work with our in-house artists, or find inspiration from watching your neighbors and friends create. 

Finish your creating and bring it along with your friends and family to COAST on September 15

Register online or sign-up to enter the contest when you get to COAST on September 15. Once registered, you'll get assigned a number/bib to wear for judging. 

Go to the review area between 10AM - 3PM so that you can be seen by our esteemed panel of judges.

  • Molly Allis

    Molly Allis is a Los Angeles-based artist, educator, filmmaker and musician who worked with the renowned Bread & Puppet theater in Vermont, as well as Great Small Works Toy Theater Company in Brooklyn. She has created animations that have won awards internationally, including at the Museum of Fine Arts in Boston, the Brooklyn Academy of Music, the Madrid Experimental Film Festival, and the Linoleum Festival in Moscow.

  • California Feet Warmers

    Based in Los Angeles, masters of ragtime, Dixieland blues and early swing (New Orleans, Circa 1920), the California Feetwarmers played with Keb’ Mo’ on his album, BluesAmericana. The collaboration won them a Grammy-nomination in the Best American Roots Performance category.

  • Cuñao

    A Los Angeles-based, Latin Folk group, Cuñao uses their multi-ethnic background and influences to put a global twist on their Latin sound by exploring African rhythms, Eastern European melodies and American Rock aesthetics. Started in the spring of 2014, their first album “Sangre Y Arena” was released in October 2015.

  • Cory Keen and Keen Ramps

    Cory Keen has been skateboarding and a strong enthusiast of the culture for over two decades. Upon completing his Master's Degree in City and Regional Planning, Keen returned to his roots and created a quarter pipe ramp as a gift for his seven-year-old nephew and thus, Keen Ramps was born.

  • jumpLA

    jumpLA specializes in high-energy jump rope performances and instruction, and features some of the top jump rope performers and coaches in the world. Founded by four-time World Champion Mike Fry, JumpLA’s interactive zone will feature single rope, couble dutch, and group freestyle jumping with music and featured performers of all ages.

  • John Steven Morgan

    An American composer and pianist, Morgan has performed at the Monterey Jazz Festival, San Francisco Davies Symphony Hall and toured internationally.

  • Jose Sainz

    Jose Sainz is the Horatio Alger of American kite making. Based in Los Angeles, Just three years after taking up the sport, he won the difficult "triple" at the American Kitefliers Association's annual festival-only the second person ever to have done so. Sainz won kite’s Grand National Championship three times.

  • Peter Tigler

    From large-scale public works viewed by thousands daily to portraits hanging in private homes, Santa Monica-based artist Peter Tigler has brought a unique vision and a variety of influences to his dramatic, recognizable pieces. Working in everything from traditional mediums like oils to cutting-edge computer imaging, Tigler has demonstrated a willingness to meet any artistic challenge.

Mission and Goals

Mobility:

COAST celebrates all the ways people get around without cars. 

The goal is to lessen our dependence on cars in order to advance the health of our environment, our bodies, and our bank accounts. 

People-powered = power to the people!

Community:

COAST empowers Santa Monicans to engage with each other and with the city around them.

The goal is to inspire those who share the city’s public spaces to connect and build, together, lasting change within the community.

Knowing and loving thy neighbor is a radical form of environmental activism. 

Creativity:

COAST participants make the event magic through their self-expression and collaboration. 

The goal is to support and facilitate the community’s collaborative imagination. We want you (yes, you!) to make something awesome!

Businesses along the route are invited to participate!

Follow the do's and don'ts outlined below for ideas on how you can activate the street in front of yout establishment during COAST. For more details on how you can paricipate, please contact CARS at kai@carsla.net or (213) 355-8506.

DO

  • Open your doors to the public during COAST!
  • Host a sidewalk sale in front of your store.
  • Offer a COAST special. Sign up an publicize your special here. 
  • Develop an interactive activity that engagess attendees. Contact us for ideas!

  • Block the entire sidewalk; a clear walking path must be maintained.
  • Post stationary signs in the public right-of-way. 
  • Have sales beyond the sidewalk. Sales are not permitted in the street. 

Getting Here

Ride the Expo Line

Metro Rail drops you off directly on the route at Colorado/4th Street. Use the Metro Trip Planner to find the best route. 

Hop on the Bus

Big Blue Bus will be free for all event participants – just show this coupon! Take routes 1, 2, 3, Rapid 3, 5, 7, Rapid 7 8, 9, an 18 all serve Downtown Santa Monica. With so many options, one probably goes by you! Find your route here.

Check out Santa Monica’s Bike Map to chart your best path to the event. Or use Google Maps if coming from outside the city.

Details

Rules

Share the Road – While cars are not allowed, most other forms of non-motorized transportation are. Be mindful of others enjoying the route with you!

Car Crossings – At a few locations along the route we’ll have designated intersections for cars to cross. Rides and pedestrians must stop at these crossings when told to do so. 

Helmets for Children – All children (18 and under) are required by law to wear a helmet if they are on a bike, scooter, skateboard, roller blades, or any other similar type of transportation. 

Electric Scooters/Bikes – Any electric assisted bicycles or scooters are limited to 5 miles per hour within the event area. All shared devices will have their throttle automatically adjusted. 

Safety

The Santa Monica Police Department provides security to the COAST event. Additionally, we’ll have emergency services stationed throughout the event. 

In the case of an accident, report the incident immediately to the nearest event staff member, emergency personnel, or uniformed officer. 

Report any suspicious activity or packages to the nearest staff member or uniformed officer. 

Staff can be identified by the t-shirt that says STAFF on the back. Volunteers can be identified by the t-shirt that says VOLUNTEER on the back.
